Star Wars canon map

Horizontal timeline from the High Republic to the First Order

This chart lays the major eras on a single horizontal axis and then places key characters as lifelines beneath it. It is schematic rather than birth-certificate precise, so the bars are meant to show story relevance across eras, not exact day-level canon dates.

High Republic Republic / Jedi fall Empire Rebellion New Republic First Order

The axis runs from roughly 100 BBY to 35 ABY, with the Battle of Yavin at 0. Negative values are BBY and positive values are ABY. Scroll sideways on smaller screens.

Reading rule: thick bars show the period where a character is active in the core story; thin continuation bars show later survival or legacy presence.

How to read Jedi

Jedi such as Yoda, Obi-Wan, Anakin, Ahsoka, Luke, and Rey do not all belong to one continuous Jedi Order. The line breaks reflect the collapse of the Republic-era Jedi, the Imperial dark age, Luke’s restoration attempt, and Rey’s later rebuilding phase.

Empire to Rebellion

Cassian Andor, Bix Caleen, and Wilmon Paak sit mainly in the late Empire years, just before the Original Trilogy. Their lifelines are concentrated near 5 BBY to 0 BBY because that is where their story matters most on screen.

Bounty hunters and Mandalorians

Boba Fett begins as a Clone Wars child, then becomes most visible during the Empire and post-Imperial years. Din Djarin belongs almost entirely to the New Republic period after Return of the Jedi.

Legacy generation

Leia, Han, Luke, and later Ben Solo bridge the Rebellion, New Republic, and First Order eras. Rey and Finn arrive at the far right because their main story sits deep in the sequel period.
